How often should I get my child’s haircut?

For boys, children's haircuts are typically recommended every 4 to 6 weeks to maintain their style. For girls with longer hair, a cut twice a year is usually sufficient, with one of those trims ideally scheduled after summer to address dry, damaged ends from swimming.

What age are kids when they first get their haircut?

It ultimately depends on personal preference. We've given haircuts as young as a few days old, but boys typically get their first haircut around age one. For girls, it's usually between ages two and three, though this can vary based on individual choice.

How often should I wash my child’s hair?

This can vary significantly based on individual hair type. Most children need their hair washed 2-4 times per week during colder seasons and daily in the summer if they’re swimming or using sunscreen.

My child hates haircuts. Can you still cut their hair?

Absolutely! It’s always up to the parents and how they want to approach their child’s haircut experience. We provide plenty of options, like our fun car chairs, or children can sit on a parent’s lap if they prefer. We’ve even had kids sit on the floor playing with toys during their haircut. We do everything we can to accommodate each child’s needs and make the experience enjoyable for everyone.

When talking to children about their appointment, it’s best to say they’re getting a “trim” rather than a “cut,” as the word "cut" might make them think of getting hurt. If they have a pacifier, comfort item, or favorite toy, we recommend bringing it along to help them feel more at ease in our suite.

You can also find plenty of positive videos on YouTube of kids getting their hair trimmed to show them what to expect. Additionally, there are many great books about getting a haircut that you can read together to prepare them.
